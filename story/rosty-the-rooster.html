Roosty, the Rooster

By: J. Jadulco

A few months ago, we were graced with a visit from my uncle. In true Filipino fashion, he brought a ‘pasalubong’ for my father - a cockfighting rooster. Both men have a shared fondness for these birds, a hobby that is quite popular in our provinces. However, our humble home was not quite ready to accommodate such an addition due to its limited space.

We initially tried to house the rooster, affectionately named ‘Rosty’, in an outdoor corner. Rosty had a knack for crowing at the crack of dawn, much to our neighbors’ dismay. I proposed an innovative solution - a ‘pay-parking’ arrangement for Rosty with a local cockfighter caretaker down the street. My father, however, dismissed this idea.

Instead, he devised an unconventional housing solution for Rosty. Each night, Rosty would be placed in a ‘bayong’ and stowed away in the cabinet under our sink until late morning - just when our neighbors would have left for work. Despite my concerns for Rosty's wellbeing, my father reassured me that chickens are capable of enduring extended periods in confined spaces.

After several days of this arrangement, my father decided it was time for Rosty to find a more suitable home. He sought the assistance of our local waste collector to find a prospective buyer. A potential sale was nearly finalized, but my niece’s attachment to Rosty prevented the transaction from being completed.

Fortunately, a local chicken enthusiast heard about our predicament and offered to house Rosty on his vacant lot. Thus, Rosty found himself relocated to a more spacious environment where he could socialize with other roosters. This amusing episode serves as a reminder of the unexpected adventures that life often presents us with.
